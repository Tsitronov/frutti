## 1. Definitions

1.1 Terms used in this DPA have the meanings given in GDPR (Regulation (EU) 2016/679) unless otherwise defined.

1.2 **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person processed under this DPA, as further specified in Annex 1.

1.3 **"Processing"** means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, retrieval, use, disclosure, erasure, or destruction.

1.4 **"Sub-processor"** means any third party engaged by the Processor to carry out processing activities on behalf of the Controller.

---

## 2. Subject Matter, Nature, and Purpose of Processing

2.1 The Processor shall process Personal Data solely for the purpose of providing the Service as described in the Main Agreement and Annex 1 of this DPA.

2.2 The Processor shall not process Personal Data for any other purpose, including its own commercial purposes, marketing, or analytics, without documented prior written instruction from the Controller.

2.3 The Processor shall process Personal Data only on the documented instructions of the Controller. If the Processor is required by EU or Member State law to process Personal Data beyond those instructions, it shall notify the Controller before such processing, unless prohibited by law on grounds of public interest.

---

## 3. Processor Obligations

The Processor shall:

3.1 **Confidentiality** — ensure that persons authorised to process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.

3.2 **Security** — implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, as set out in Annex 2, including:
  - Encryption of Personal Data in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256 or equivalent);
  - Ability to ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems;
  - Ability to restore availability and access to Personal Data in a timely manner in the event of a physical or technical incident;
  - A process for regularly testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of technical and organisational measures.

3.3 **Sub-processors** — not engage any Sub-processor without the prior specific or general written authorisation of the Controller. Current authorised Sub-processors are listed in Annex 3. The Processor shall impose data-protection obligations on any Sub-processor equivalent to those set out in this DPA and shall remain fully liable to the Controller for any failure by a Sub-processor.

3.4 **Data subject rights** — assist the Controller, by appropriate technical and organisational measures, insofar as possible, in fulfilling the Controller's obligation to respond to requests from data subjects exercising their rights under Chapter III GDPR (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection).

3.5 **Controller obligations** — assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with Articles 32–36 GDPR (security, breach notification, DPIA, prior consultation).

3.6 **Deletion or return** — at the Controller's choice, upon termination of the Main Agreement:
  - delete all Personal Data and certify deletion in writing within 30 days; or
  - return all Personal Data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format within 30 days,
  unless EU or Member State law requires storage.

3.7 **Audits** — make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this Article and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller. The Processor may require reasonable prior notice (minimum 14 days) and may charge reasonable costs for such audits.

3.8 **Data breach notification** — notify the Controller without undue delay, and in any event within **48 hours**, after becoming aware of a Personal Data breach. The notification shall include, to the extent then available:
  - description of the nature of the breach;
  - categories and approximate number of data subjects affected;
  - categories and approximate number of Personal Data records affected;
  - likely consequences of the breach;
  - measures taken or proposed to address the breach.

## 4. Controller Obligations

4.1 The Controller shall ensure that it has a valid legal basis for the Processing and that data subjects have been informed of the Processing in accordance with Articles 13–14 GDPR.

4.2 The Controller shall ensure that the Personal Data provided to the Processor is accurate, adequate, and limited to what is necessary.

4.3 The Controller is responsible for defining and providing documented instructions to the Processor.

## ANNEX 2 — Technical and Organisational Security Measures

| Measure | Implementation |
|---|---|
| Encryption in transit | TLS 1.2+ (HTTPS enforced) |
| Password storage | bcrypt hashing (cost factor ≥ 12) |
| Authentication tokens | JWT access token in memory; refresh token in HttpOnly, Secure, SameSite=Strict cookie |
| Access control | Role-based (viewer / staff / admin) |
| Rate limiting | Max 5 login attempts per 15 minutes per IP |
| Security headers | X-Frame-Options: DENY, X-Content-Type-Options: nosniff, Referrer-Policy, Permissions-Policy |
| Audit logging | Server access logs retained 30 days |
| Breach detection | Monitoring via hosting provider; manual review process |
| Data minimisation | Only fields necessary for care management are collected |
| Backups | Managed by Render.com with daily snapshots |
