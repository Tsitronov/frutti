## 1. Definitions

1.1 **"HIPAA"** means the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, as amended by the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health (HITECH) Act (42 U.S.C. §17921 et seq.) and all implementing regulations.

1.2 **"PHI"** (Protected Health Information) means individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form or medium that relates to the past, present, or future physical or mental health or condition of an individual, the provision of health care to an individual, or the past, present, or future payment for health care. PHI includes electronic PHI ("ePHI").

1.3 **"Privacy Rule"** means the Standards for Privacy of Individually Identifiable Health Information, 45 CFR Part 164, Subparts A and E.

1.4 **"Security Rule"** means the Security Standards for the Protection of Electronic PHI, 45 CFR Part 164, Subparts A and C.

1.5 **"Breach"** has the meaning given at 45 CFR §164.402.

1.6 **"Unsecured PHI"** has the meaning given at 45 CFR §164.402.

1.7 Other terms used but not defined herein have the meanings given in HIPAA.

---

## 2. Permitted Uses and Disclosures of PHI by BA

2.1 **Service performance.** BA may use and disclose PHI as necessary to provide the services described in the Service Agreement on behalf of CE.

2.2 **Operations of BA.** BA may use PHI for the proper management and administration of BA's business or to carry out BA's legal responsibilities, provided that:
  - The disclosure is required by law; or
  - BA obtains reasonable assurances from any recipient that PHI will be held confidentially and used or further disclosed only as required by law or for the purpose for which it was disclosed, and that any breach will be reported to BA.

2.3 **Minimum necessary.** BA shall use, disclose, or request only the minimum necessary PHI to accomplish the intended purpose, consistent with 45 CFR §164.502(b).

2.4 **Prohibition.** BA shall not use or disclose PHI in any manner that would violate the Privacy Rule if done by CE, except as permitted in Sections 2.1–2.2 above.

2.5 **No sale of PHI.** BA shall not sell PHI or use PHI for marketing purposes without the CE's prior written authorisation and individual authorisation where required by HIPAA.

---

## 3. BA Obligations

3.1 **Safeguards.** BA shall implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI created, received, maintained, or transmitted on behalf of CE, as required by the Security Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C). BA's current safeguards are described in Exhibit A.

3.2 **Agents and Sub-contractors.** BA shall ensure that any agent or sub-contractor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of BA agrees to the same restrictions, conditions, and requirements that apply to BA under this BAA. A list of authorised sub-contractors is provided in Exhibit B.

3.3 **Access to PHI.** BA shall make PHI available to CE and, as directed by CE, to individuals, in accordance with 45 CFR §164.524 (right of access).

3.4 **Amendment.** BA shall make PHI available for amendment and incorporate any amendments directed by CE in accordance with 45 CFR §164.526.

3.5 **Accounting of disclosures.** BA shall maintain and make available the information required for CE to provide an accounting of disclosures as required by 45 CFR §164.528 for a period of **six (6) years** from the date of the disclosure.

3.6 **Internal practices.** BA shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) for the purpose of determining CE's compliance with HIPAA.

3.7 **Return or destruction of PHI.** Upon termination of this BAA for any reason, BA shall, at CE's election:
  - Return all PHI to CE within 30 days; or
  - Destroy all PHI and certify in writing that PHI has been destroyed.
  If return or destruction is not feasible, BA shall extend the protections of this BAA to the retained PHI and limit further uses and disclosures to those purposes that make the return or destruction infeasible.

## 4. Breach Notification

4.1 **Discovery.** A Breach shall be treated as discovered by BA on the first day on which the Breach is known, or by exercising reasonable diligence would have been known, to any person (other than the person committing the Breach) who is an employee, officer, or other agent of BA.

4.2 **Notification to CE.** BA shall notify CE of a Breach of Unsecured PHI without unreasonable delay and in no event later than **10 calendar days** after discovery of the Breach. The notification shall include, to the extent possible:
  - Identification of the individuals whose Unsecured PHI has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed;
  - Brief description of the Breach, including date of occurrence and date of discovery;
  - Description of the types of Unsecured PHI involved;
  - Steps individuals should take to protect themselves;
  - Brief description of what BA is doing to investigate, mitigate harm, and prevent recurrence;
  - Contact information for BA.

4.3 **CE responsibility.** CE is responsible for notifying affected individuals, HHS, and (if applicable) the media in accordance with 45 CFR §§164.404–164.408. BA shall reasonably cooperate with CE in its notification obligations.

4.4 **Unsuccessful access.** BA shall also report to CE any attempted or successful unauthorised access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of PHI that does not constitute a Breach but of which BA becomes aware.
